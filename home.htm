Merry Christmas 2008
12-12-08
Dear Friends,
Another year has past and once again it has gone by way too fast! It’s been another year of ups and downs, but through it all there has always been one constant, and that is our wonderful Lord! The children have grown so much and I no longer have a “little baby”. Even though Avery will always be my baby, I’m definitely not allowed to call him that anymore. If I do he always corrects me with “I not a baby, I Avery!” We are still homeschooling and I must admit, it has definitely been a good thing this year with all of Jon’s traveling. We’ve been able to pack up all our books and go with him to Pensacola, FL. He’s been doing work down here pretty much since August. His first trip down,we spent 5 out of the 8 weeks he was gone apart and learned very quickly that being together, no matter where it was, was much better. It’s been hard being away from all our family, but we’re just so thankful to have had the opportunity to go with him! We can’t wait to be home for Christmas! Molly is 9, and is such a wonderful help to me. She loves babies and especially loves to take care of her little cousin Lincoln. Emily is 8 and a sweetheart. She enjoys organizing play time with Evan, Avery and cousin Owen. She has a very big imagination and loves to pretend! Evan is a little more timid with things and tends to be a little more wary of people and new places, but give him a chance to warm up and he’s great! Avery on the other hand, has not an ounce of fear in his body. But thankfully the Lord made him with a really hard head ( and I don’t mean stubborn, though he is that as well, but literally, a hard head) I stand in awe as I watch him jump from something or fall from something and I wait for the cry that never comes! He just jumps up and keeps on going! All the children are such a blessing and I’m so thankful for each of them, (as well as their amazing and wonderful father!!!)

It’s crazy how one little six letter word can so change your lives, but as most of you know, CANCER reared it’s ugly head once more this year, this time attacking my mom.

Just like with Ethan though, it shocks you, sends you reeling not knowing which way is up and then suddenly God’s wonderful peace quiets and calms, and then you focus on the battle aspect, and do what we have to do to beat this thing. My mom was chosen for a study and has done incredibly well with it. She has had some really hard days though. It’s so hard to watch and know that you really can’t do anything to make things better. But even on her “bad days”, if you were to talk to her on the phone, you would have no clue that there was anything wrong with her. She has amazing strength and grace and beauty through all that she is going through, and for all of that we give the glory and honor to the Lord! We will definitely be celebrating this Christmas! Mother had her last chemo treatment on December 10th, and though she still has a ways to go with surgery and radiation, it is so wonderful to not have any more of that horrible chemo!

Ethan is never far from our thoughts. In our memories he will always be our sweet baby boy and as I remember holding him and seeing his precious little smile, I think of Mary. Did she truly understand that the baby boy that she held in her arms that night so long ago was going to be the Saviour of all mankind? What a precious gift! I am so thankful for all that has been given to us. The Lord is forever faithful. We wish you all a very Merry Christmas and a wonderful New Year!
In His Love,
Caroline, Jon, Molly, Emily, Evan, and Avery Smith

Dear Praying Friends,
04-28-05
Our little Ethan is now in the presence of the Lord. To be honest with you, if anyone had told us that we would have never brought him home after this last visit to Charleston, we would have said they were crazy!! But it is truly amazing to see how the Lord had His hand in the whole situation.

Ethan had the surgery on Thursday and that is when the doctors discovered just how big the tumor was and how much damage it had already caused. They said the tumor was about half of Ethans body weight which would make it about 7 lbs. The left lobe of the liver they found out had scar tissue and could not be saved. So it came down to this, we could try other types of experimental chemo to shrink the tumor and still try to remove it, but he would still have to have a liver transplant after that.

The problem we would run into at that point is, if they didn't get all the cancer cells, as soon as he started the anti-rejection drugs from the transplant, they would tell his body that every thing was fine, and then the cancer would spread like wild fire. Within only a couple of months we would be right back to where we started, only poor little Ethan would have endured so much. We made the decision to not go any farther with treatments. We were going to get Ethan home just as soon as we could and just love him and take care of him until the Lord took him home. Once again though, the Lord helped us through a very difficult situation.

The thought of bringing him home and not knowing how long he would have to suffer before he died was the hardest thing for both Jon and I. We knew that He would give us that measure of grace when the time came, but instead, He didn't make us go through it at all.

On Friday night, our Dr. from the PICU took us aside and told us that there was a good chance that Ethan would not come off the ventilator. They had been trying to wean him off all day, but with no success. He said that they wanted to try to take him off between 8-10:00 the next morning. We called all of our family to prepare them for the possibilty of his death, and then prayed for God's Will to be done. We had such wonderful doctors and nurses while we were there. They were so thoughtful and compassionate towards us and we will be forever grateful to them for all they did.

At about 9:30 on Sat. morning they took the tube out. We had made the decision to not put it back if he was having trouble. The doctors said that they could keep him on it 4-5 days, but didn't feel that the outcome would be any different. After they took him off the ventilator, they took out all his IV's and closed the curtain around us, brought in a rocking chair and put Ethan in my arms. He struggled for a few minutes, but then calmed right down. Jon and I held him and rocked him and sang to him. He was so peaceful and sweet! His breathing began to slow down and as I rested my hand over his heart, it began to beat slower and slower until it just stopped. We held him for an hour and a half. It seemed like only minutes. If anyone would have told me that I could hold my little baby as he died, I would have said there's no way. It would be too hard. But I feel that I need to share the final minutes that we had with him because it was so sweet and so peaceful. Jon said it so sweetly, "how wonderful it was to see Ethan go from his mothers arms into our Heavenly Fathers arms." The Lord was so merciful to us to take Ethan so quickly.

Jon and I have an amazing peace even still. We miss Ethan of course, but I think what has made it so much easier is the fact that we never prayed for God to heal him. We could only pray that the Lord's perfect will would be done. Jon and I had to pray without certain expectations, because if we said that we just knew that Ethan would be OK, it would just open us up to be bitter towards God for not answering our prayers.

But he did answer our prayers. His will was done. Ethan's purpose in life was fulfilled, it just happened to be in 8 1/2 short months. Jon and I are so thankful for the time that we had with Ethan. He was such a blessing and we love him so much. We are now ready to go on though. We have 3 beautiful children who need our love and attention. The girls have handled things very well. It's hard, but God is also giving them the grace they need whether they know it or not. Thank you all for you thoughts and prayers, emails and letters, meals and gifts.

The Lord has used each and every one of you in our lives, I wish we could thank each one of you personally for everything. I especially want to thank our doctors and nurses who God placed in our lives. You are all so special! When you spend as much time in doctors offices and hopsitals as we have, you really rely on the care and support offered by each one involved. There was no one involved with little Ethan's care that God did not place there, and for that we are so grateful!

The Lord has used this in our lives to draw us close to Him as well as to each other. I am so thankful to Jon for his love and support. He's such a wonderful daddy and husband. The Lord has showed His love and mercy over and over. Whenever there was a decision to be made that was too hard, He just closed that door and led us another direction. It's just amazing to see now, after being on the other side of this whole ordeal, how God's hand led us all the way.

In His Love,
Caroline and Jon

News from MUSC
04-14-05 pm
I was called back from MUSC a little while ago. The doctors met yesterday and have decided that we can't do a Live Donor Transplant. The vein that is in the middle of the liver they feel is either being compressed by the tumor or the tumor is around it. There is another group of doctors meeting tomorrow at 3:00. They handle more tumors and will be able to give us a definite answer on this.

Ethan was not able to have a bone scan done yesterday because they were concerned about sedating him for so long with just the normal sedation drug (the test takes about 1 1/2 to 2 hrs) We couldn't get anything scheduled again until Monday, here in Greenville. He will have to be put under by an anesthesiologist. After it is confirmed that the cancer has not spread to his bones then he can be put on the donor list right away.

His chest CT scan came out clear for which we are thankful, and it showed that the tumor has not grown at all since the last scan. His bones are probably fine, since it hasn't spread to his lungs, but they just have to be sure!

We will probably leave on Mon. evening to go to Charleston where Ethan will be put into the ICU while we wait for a donor. We will wait about 2 weeks and if nothing becomes available, then we will start another round of chemo. We will then have to wait until he's on the upside of that until we can try for another liver.

The Lord is still giving such a peace! He's taking a lot of the guesswork out of it for us by removing options. So for that, we really are so thankful! It just helps us to see and remember that Ethan is truly in His hands. Nothing will touch him that God has not already planned. Back to Greenville
04-14-05 am
WOW is about all I can say of the past 3 days!! Thank you so much for all your prayers! They were much needed and felt!

On Monday, our morning started at 6:30 and ended that evening at 6:30 with no breakfast,lunch or even snack breaks!! We met with doctors, surgeons, nurses and social workers. Ethan had 2 ultrasounds, a chest X-ray, lab work and an EKG. They fit 4 days of tests into the one day.

We are really thankful though because I know alot of strings were pulled to accomplish what we did! On Tuesday I had to meet with my doctor, and social worker and have an ultrasound.

I still have to have a few more tests to determine if they can use my liver (Jon really wanted to be the donor, but the Dr. took one look at him and said "nope, too big!!") They, for obvious reasons have to use the smaller liver!

While I was busy on Tues. Jon stayed with Ethan while he had a blood transfusion. His blood count had gone down to 7 and they don't like to see it go below 8. They were so nice there and had no problem getting him taken care of.

We then had to leave straight from there on Tues. to be home early Wed. morning for Ethan to have a chest/abdomen CT scan and a bone scan at Greenville Memorial. They weren't able to schedule those tests in Charleston. We were also able to meet with our Dr. and have a few more questions answered.

As of Wed. night I still have not heard how the committee meeting went today in Charleston, but I'm going to try to simply let you know what our options are and the decisions that will have to be made in the next 2 weeks.

1. Determination of the main artery that goes through the middle of Ethan's liver. If the tumor is around it all then a Live Donor transplant cannot even be done, because he will need a whole liver with that vein in it.

2. Testing on my liver to be sure it can be used. The donor liver must be perfect. If mine can't be used, Live donor is also out of the question.

3. After talking with our Dr. today, we feel that we have 2 weeks to wait for a regular transplant (not involving me) Ethan will be put on the donor list ASAP, but if no donors come in and my liver is able to be used, we will go ahead and do the Live Donor transplant at that time.

4. If we get to 2 weeks and I can't donate my liver and there has not been a donor, then we really have no choice but to do another round of chemo to keep the tumor from spreading and growing. However, if this is done and a liver becomes available during the chemo, he won't be able to have the transplant. He has to wait until he's on the upside of the chemo before anything can be done.

5. There is also the possibility of the Dr's saying a transplant can't even be done. If that is the case, then Ethan will go ahead and finish the 4 rounds of chemo, and then they will try experimental drugs that are much stronger and therefore it would be a much higher complication risk for him. We were assured by our Dr. today that a transplant right now is a much better plan than the experimental drugs. They will only be our last resort.

I will try to post new news as soon as we know something! Again thank you so much for your prayers! We love you all! »top of page

04-09-05
On Good Friday, Jon and I sat in the Doctors office and listened as the Dr. confirmed what we already felt. The tumor was not shrinking from the chemo this time at all.

I asked him if the tumor could just be removed and was told that it couldn't because of all the blood vessels involved with the liver. We left feeling like we had reached the end of the road. Jon and I never really admitted it to each other at the time, nor did we tell anyone else that we felt that Ethan didn't really have a chance.

Then came Monday! A new doctor, a new day! This is when the option of a liver transplant was presented and this time we left the office feeling overjoyed! It was literally like seeing the light at the end of the tunnel!!

It has been almost 2 weeks since then, and things have moved rather quickly (which we are very excited about) Jon and I go to the Medical University of S.C. in Charleston on Monday, April 11th,where we will start the process of the liver transplant. They have condensed the various tests and meetings that what would normally take 4 days and squeezed it into one day.

They will be testing Jon and I as well to see who will be the best donor for him. One of us will give just a portion of our liver to Ethan . He won't have to go through anymore chemo once the tumor is removed.

You will see from the newest pictures the change in him from this last round of chemo. He looks tired all the time because the muscles in his eyes are so weak they just won't open all the way.
